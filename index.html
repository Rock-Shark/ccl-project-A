[*Nian-Nian-Mold*]

 

Scientific Name:

ASTree Mold

Discovery Information:

Discovered by: Angelica Sha

Location: Discarded drafts

Date: 10/24/2024

 

Family:

Fuligo.

 

 

Appearance:

The mycelium is composed of reddish-brown circular stacks that continuously contract to facilitate breathing.

When it detects nearby mycelia, it extends branches to connect and integrate with one another, forming a cohesive communicative network.

When reaching a considerate number, the pattern of the branches and myceliums resembles a tree laden with fruit, which is how it got its name.

Habitat:

Discarded drafts filled with abandoned ideas or inspirations.

The mold thrives on those neglected artistic thoughts.

Key Characteristics:

  • Where there's abandoned thoughts, there's AST.
  • Thrives on inspirations.
  • Uses branches to communicate.
  • Can find other AST molds however far they are.
  • Multiplies when there's new thoughts.
  • Not poisonous but not delicious either.

What we know so far about [*Nian-Nian-Mold*]?

It's known to all that humans are constantly forgetting thoughts. This raises a profound question that has puzzled mankind for thousands of years: Where do these forgotten thoughts go? Thanks to the groundbreaking discovery by Dr. Sha, I am delighted to introduce to you the very organism that feeds on our discarded thoughts: ASTree Mold. This microorganism thrives wherever forgotten ideas gather—whether in drafts, memos, notebooks, or even diaries. The mold’s color and shape subtly change depending on the thoughts it is currently feeding on. Furthermore, a special method is required to see this elusive creature , so no wonder humans have ignored our closest friend for such a long time: Our world would be filled with unused thoughts without these helpful decomposers！


Guidelines for human interaction with this creature

[!!TRY TO PRESS YOUR MOUSE OUTSIDE THE CANVAS!!]

Press your mouse anywhere to attract another Nian-Nian-Mold and see how these two molds communicate.

You can try drawing your own patterns (or even creating your own art!) on the canvas.

However, which mold the new-born mold chooses to communicate is randomly generated. Be patient and be nice to them.
